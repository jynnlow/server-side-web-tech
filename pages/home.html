A perpetual learner of the world driven by an insatiable curiosity. Undaunted of what lies beyond the comfort zone for raw experiences, I am always setting new travel goals. Travelling has allowed me to witness the beauties and tragedies of this world which were transformative to my outlooks. What I eventually discovered is the gap between our perspectives and reality on the ground in places beyond the borders. Through storytelling, I hope to forge connection, especially in a time when humanity is overshadowed by dooming media portrayal of the outside world.

About Juvena Huang

Juvena found her ticket to freedom when she bought her first scooter on her 20th birthday. Combining her love for riding and travel, a friend’s sudden passing drove Juvena to leave the safety net of home and job to wander the world on the scooter.

Solo but hardly alone, she eventually rode from Singapore to Europe covering 44,000km, 25 countries in 27 months. Her journey has amassed a following on the Facebook Page “The Wandering Wasp” as she shares her intimate travel experiences and reveals on the ground aspects of countries hardly shown by mainstream media. With the power of story telling, Juvena hopes to foster cross-border understanding and tolerance and to inspire others to discover their potential.

Her ambitious journey was featured in Singapore publications like The Straits Times, Today, Carbuyer and Her World.

She was interviewed by overseas published motorcycle adventurers like Lois Pryce for Bikes Magazine (UK) and Nathan Millward for Getting into Adventure: A Collection of Stories to Inspire (UK), and featured in the photobook “I Can. I Will. Women Overlanding The World.” She was even mentioned as one of the 5 Solo Female Travel Bloggers who are Challenging Stereotypes by Popsugar Culture.

The Wandering Wasp was thrown into international spotlight when she was featured in Dear Alyne’s video “Where Women Can’t Go.”

She has plans to explore Western Europe, Africa, and the America continents next.
